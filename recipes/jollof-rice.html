Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a popular west African dish that is made with spices, peppers, seasoning and rice. It is usually served with sides like deep fried steak, grilled fish, chicken and optionally plantain. This recipe makes about 3 servings of the meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long grain parboiled rice.
  • 1/8 cup of vegetable or sunflower oil.
  • 400g canned or packaged tomato.
  • 11/2 large or 3 medium sized onions.
  • 4 large red paprika.
  • 2 scotch bonnet pepper (or some other hot pepper).
  • 2 cups of chicken or beef broth.
  • 2 garlic cloves.
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste.
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder.
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme leaves.
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika.

Steps

  1. Blend the peppers and tomato and 2 medium or 1 large onions.
  2. Cut up the remaining onion and sauté on medium to low heat for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add oil and the tomato paste and fry with the onions for a few more minutes.
  4. Add ginger and garlic and continue cooking with the tomato paste for another two minutes. This makes the base sauce for the rice.
  5. Turn the heat to low and add the blended peppers.
  6. Turn the heat to medium high and cook for about 10 minutes. You can tell the sauce is ready when the pepper is deeply fried and the oil stays above the sauce.
  7. Add thyme, curry powder, salt, paprika and other spice of your choice.
  8. Add the broth to the sauce, and mix both together. Then turn the heat to low.
  9. Add the rice to the sauce and mix both together. Stir for a few minutes until no sauce is left in the pot.
  10. Cover the pot bring to a boil. Then turn the heat to medium-low.
  11. Cook until the rice becomes soft.
  12. Serve with a side of your choice.
